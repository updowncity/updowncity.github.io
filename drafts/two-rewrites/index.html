octahedral axiom (of triangulated categories)

For any morphism $f:T\to S$ in the underlying triangulated category $D$, complete the (distinguished triangle) $T\to S\to R\xrightarrow{+1}$. If there exists a decomposition of $f$, i.e. $\exists X\in\mathrm{ob}(D)$ and $T\xrightarrow{\nu}X\xrightarrow{\gamma}S$ such that $f=\gamma\circ\nu$, then complete the (distinguished) triangles induced by $\nu$ and $\gamma$, i.e. $T \xrightarrow{\nu} X \xrightarrow{\gamma’} S’\xrightarrow{+1}$ and $T’ \xrightarrow{\nu’} X \xrightarrow{\gamma} S\xrightarrow{+1}$. Do the same operation again, complete the (distinguished) triangle induced by $f’=\gamma’\circ\nu’$, i.e. $T’ \xrightarrow{\gamma’\circ\nu’}S’ \xrightarrow{ y} R’\xrightarrow{+1}$. Then what the octahedral says is just $R=R’$.

homotopy category

To aviod potential confusing, let’s begin with minimum requirement for building a homotopy category. Suppose $\mathcal{A}$ is an exact category such that for any two objects $A$ and $A’$ in $\mathcal{A}$, we have a predefined structure of abelian groups (or $\mathbb{Z}$-module, if you think this will make things more clear) in each $\mathrm{Hom}_\mathcal{A}(A,A’)$, and $\mathrm{Kom}(\mathcal{A})$ be the category of chain complexes with values in $\mathcal{A}$.

The classical definition of homtopy category is written by setting an equivalence relation $\sim$ in each $\mathrm{Hom}(A^\bullet,B^\bullet)$, i.e. for any $f^\bullet,g^\bullet\in \mathrm{Hom}(A^\bullet,B^\bullet)$, morphisms $f^\bullet\sim g^\bullet$ if and only if there exists a family of morphisms $s^i\in\mathrm{Hom}(A^i,B^{i-1})$ in such that $f^i-g^i=s^{i+1}\circ d_{A^\bullet}+d_{B^\bullet}^{i-1}\circ s^i$ in $\mathcal{A}$.

Obversed that if we rewrite each homotopy into $s^i: A^i\to B[-1]^i$, then the equivalence relation $\sim$ becomes $f^i-g^i=s^{i+1}\circ d_{A^\bullet}^i-d_{B^\bullet[-1]}^i\circ s^i$. (Let $d^i_{B[-1]}:=-d_B^{i-1}$ just like what we do when defining triangulated categories.)

Force $f^\bullet$ equivalent to $g^\bullet$ just like making $(s^i)_{i\in\mathbb{Z}}$ into a morphism between chain complexes, i.e.

For a morphism $f^\bullet: A^\bullet\to B^\bullet$ we no longer require that the requirement for local commutative square $f^{i+1}\circ d_{A^\bullet}^i-d_{B^\bullet[-1]}^i\circ f^i$.
